OBSERVATIONS FOR golden_set.csv
===========================================================================

  1. Only 1 genuine ML/ranking/retrieval engineer in 50 candidates (CAND_0000031).
     This is realistic — the JD says "narrow profile, we expect 10 great in 100K."

  2. Most of the sample are trap/honeypot-style profiles: wrong titles, consulting
     firms, keyword-stuffed skills with no career evidence.

  3. Key discriminators we must get right in feature_builder.py:
     a. Title vs career description alignment — a "Frontend Engineer" with FAISS
        skills is a trap; the description must confirm the skill.
     b. Company type penalty must apply to entire career, not just current role.
     c. Open_to_work + response_rate + last_active together determine reachability.
        All three must be combined, not checked individually.
     d. Notice period >90 days is a strong downranking signal.

  4. The data appears to have profile-description mismatches (same description
     repeated across different roles in CAND_0000031). This is likely a synthetic
     data artifact. We should NOT penalise for repeated descriptions across roles —
     it's a data generation artifact, not a honeypot signal.

  5. Candidates from outside India without willing_to_relocate need strong downgrade.
     JD does not sponsor visas and is India-preferred.
